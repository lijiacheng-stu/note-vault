01 Definition and Characteristics
What is Social Behavior?
Social behavior is how people act when interacting with others or being influenced by society.
What is the key characteristics of Social Behavior?
Interaction-based:Social behavior always involves two or more individuals
Context-dependent:Behavior changes depending on culture, situation and group.
Reciprocal:One person’s behavior affects another’s, and vice versa.

02 Examples of social behavior
Conformity:Changing behavior to match a group
Follow the queue,Agree with the majority opinion
Obedience:Following instructions from authority.
Buy toothpaste recommended by experts,Students following teachers
Competition:Trying to win against others.
Olympics，E-sport，contest

03 The functions of social behavior
The functions of social behavior refer to using the characteristics and classic examples of social behavior to achieve certain goals.
1 Maintain social order
•Governments make laws (Obedience)
•Teachers and parents educate us to obey rules(Obedience)
•Friends and classmates follow the law together(Conformity)
2 Advertise
•Create fake orders to boost sales. (Conformity)
•Celebrities and scientists endorse products. (Obedience)
•Setting levels and ranks to encourage players to buy more items. (competition)
3 Build relationships
•Greet and help others. (Reciprocal)
•Know this person‘s past so that know what is encouraged to say and do. (Context-dependent)
•Participate in activities in which this person take part. (Interaction-based)

05 Our attitude toward social behavior
We are both receivers and influencers of social behavior. In other words, We are influenced by social behavior, and we also influence others through social behavior. Therefore, we should be aware, critical, and responsible.
1. We are influenced by social behavior
●As citizens, customers, and friends, our minds and decisions are shaped by others.
●We should think critically. Is the product quality really as good as advertised? Do I really need it? Is this friendship genuine?
2. We influence others through social behavior
●Our actions affect people around us
●We should act responsibly, Ensure your goals and methods are socially and morally acceptable.
